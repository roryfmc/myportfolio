The Spatial Web
Gabriel Rene, Dan Mapes
****
My recent interests have shifted towards Web3 and I wanted to learn more. If anyone Is looking to learn more on the topic this is the book. This book explains the idea of web3 and what It involves as well as the steps needed In order to transition from Web2 - Web3. It also touches on the negative uses of Web3 and how It could lead to this dystopian future. The book opened my eyes to the future of not only tech but the world and the possible technologies that will exist in the future.
How To Teach Quantum Physics To Your Dog
Chad Orzel
****
As someone who despised Physics in school this book made for a very enjoyable read and gave me an insight to quantum mechanics. It wasn't a hard read in terms of dense content but more trying to wrap your head round particular theories. The role of the dog helps massively and as someone not particularly versed in physics helped massively in understanding the concepts put forth. I also read this book to say I was reading a book about quantum physics. Good book.
Welcome to Blackwater: Mercenaries, Money and Mayhem in Iraq
Morgan Lerette
*****
The book Is a first person true account of his experience as a Blackwater Operative in Iraq. Apart from the book being juvenile and some what immature. The detail and expeirences in this book are incredibly interesting. It was the first funny book I've read and would recommend reading It as long as you keep In mind that Its raw and meant to be juvenile, leading to a very honest book about his experince.
The Shadow World: Inside the Global Arms Trade
Andrew Feinstein
****
It is practically a historical account of most of the major arms trades as well as corruption within governments. The book opened my eyes to the sheer scale and money revolving around the arms trade. The only fault to this book is its length however, the amount of different and invaluable content in this book makes up for it. At times it was a bit of a hard read just because of the vasts amount of information. The author doesn't hold back and names everyone. If time I would read.
The Critical Handbook Of Money Laundering
Petrus C.Van Duyne, Jackie H.Harvey, Liliya Y.Gelemerova
*
It was a hard and dense read, I stopped half-way because it felt never ending. I think when I was reading this book and 'Fundamentals of Offshore Banking' I may have been going through a phase. I think reading these books got the thought of money laundering or offshore banking out of my system just because they were so lifeless.
Fundamentals of Offshore Banking
Walter Tyndale
**
Dry read with little pay off. I dont really know what I was hoping from this book, maybe something more secretive? The book didn't really delve into specifcs. It was more a large overview of potential methods. This book will not help you evade taxes.
The Polymath
Peter Burke
**
After reading Waqas Ahmed's 'The PolyMath'. I wanted to explore the meaning of what it meant to be a polymath. This book was more a historical account of previous polymaths and putting Leonardo Da Vinci on a pedestal.
The PolyMath
Waqas Ahmed
***
Tough read considering it was the book that led to me begin reading. Tends to ramble on and was different to my expectations as to what I thought the book would be. I think the title interested me more than the actual contents of the book. As a computer student it gave an insight to how important being a polymath is to have a career in this field and standing out.
